Hands-On Care for Stress, Recovery, and Comfort

2) How do these treatments work?

A Personalized Session, Not a One‑Size‑Fits‑All Routine

Process clarity reduces hesitation and improves satisfaction. When clients understand sequence, timing, and customization options in advance, the appointment feels predictable and professional. This also helps set realistic expectations for what changes immediately, what improves gradually, and what habits support better continuity between visits.

Every visit starts with a quick, practical check-in. We want to understand what you’re feeling that day and what you want to get out of the session—relaxation, recovery, pain relief, or overall wellness support.

  • Consultation: goals, pressure preferences, problem areas
  • Comfort setup: positioning, draping, and temperature
  • Customized technique: Swedish, deep tissue, sports, Ashiatsu, or prenatal approach
  • Targeted focus: neck, shoulders, back, hips, legs, or other tension patterns
  • Add-ons (optional): hot stone, CBD, cupping, aromatherapy, paraffin
  • Aftercare: hydration, simple guidance, and ideal rebooking cadence

Swedish vs. Deep Tissue: Swedish uses flowing strokes to calm the nervous system and reduce general tension. Deep tissue is more focused and pressure-driven, targeting deeper layers of tightness—ideal when you feel “knots” that do not release easily.

Prenatal massage safety: Prenatal massage is performed with supportive positioning and a comfort-first approach. If you are pregnant, let us know at booking so we can plan the safest and most appropriate session structure for you.

Lymphatic work: This is typically lighter pressure and often booked when guests want to feel less “puffy,” support circulation, and feel more reset overall. It is not the same as deep tissue—think soothing and supportive, not intense.
