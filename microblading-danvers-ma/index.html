1) What is Microblading & Permanent Makeup?

Semi-Permanent Enhancements for Brows and Lips

Most guests need more than a definition - they need clarity on fit. This service is usually best when your current goals, schedule, and comfort level align with the treatment design. That is why we frame this section around who benefits most, what realistic outcomes look like, and how this service compares to common alternatives people consider before booking.

Microblading and permanent makeup are cosmetic tattoo techniques designed to enhance features with long-lasting pigment. The goal is to create a soft, natural-looking improvement—so you look more defined and put together even with minimal makeup.

  • Microblading: fine, hair-like strokes for brows that mimic natural brow hairs.
  • Microshading: a soft “powder” effect for brows, adding fullness and definition with a more filled-in finish.
  • Lip Blush: a sheer, natural-looking tint to enhance lip color and shape.
  • Lip Neutralization: a color-correction approach designed to balance uneven or darker lip tones.

Microblading vs. microshading: Microblading is more “hair-stroke” and airy. Microshading is more “soft makeup” and density. Many guests choose based on their brow goals, skin type, and how bold they want the result to feel.

Most results are considered semi-permanent. Longevity varies by individual, but many guests plan on periodic refreshes to keep color looking its best over time.

2) How do these treatments work?

Consultation, Mapping, Application, Aftercare

Process clarity reduces hesitation and improves satisfaction. When clients understand sequence, timing, and customization options in advance, the appointment feels predictable and professional. This also helps set realistic expectations for what changes immediately, what improves gradually, and what habits support better continuity between visits.

Permanent makeup is a detailed process. At Spalenza Spa, the goal is to make each step clear, comfortable, and intentional—especially if it’s your first time.

  • Consultation: discuss goals, style preference, and review suitability.
  • Mapping & design: brows or lips are mapped for shape and balance before pigment is applied.
  • Pigment selection: choose tones that complement your skin tone and natural coloring.
  • Application: microblading strokes, shading technique, or lip pigment application depending on service.
  • Healing & aftercare: a structured healing period where results soften and settle.
  • Touch-up planning: many clients benefit from a follow-up visit to refine results after healing.

What to expect: immediately after your appointment, brows or lips may look stronger than the final healed result. Over the healing period, color typically softens and becomes more natural-looking.

Aftercare matters. The quality of your healed result depends heavily on following aftercare instructions, avoiding picking, and protecting the area as it heals. We’ll guide you clearly so you know what to do (and what to avoid).

3) Why get Microblading & Permanent Makeup?

Less Daily Effort, More Daily Confidence

The strongest value is usually both visible and practical. Beyond appearance improvements, clients often choose this service to reduce routine friction, improve confidence in daily life, and maintain results with less stress. Looking at lifestyle benefit and visual benefit together gives a more accurate picture of long-term return.

People don’t book permanent makeup because they want to look “done-up.” They book because they want to feel more consistent in their appearance without spending extra time every morning.

  • Time saved: less brow and lip makeup every day
  • More symmetry: a balanced shape that frames your face
  • Low-maintenance definition: polished look for work, gym, and weekends
  • Confidence: feel more put together on no-makeup days
  • Travel-friendly: less packing and less touch-up stress

How long do results last? Longevity varies by skin type, lifestyle, sun exposure, and aftercare. Many clients enjoy results for months to years and then choose a refresh when color softens. During your consultation, we’ll talk through realistic expectations based on the service you choose.

If you’re unsure whether to choose microblading or microshading, think about your preferred finish: hair-like softness vs. a fuller powder look. If you want lips that look naturally tinted and more even, lip blush or neutralization may be the right direction.
