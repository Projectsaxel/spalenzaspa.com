1) What are Advanced Facials & Chemical Peels?

Professional Treatments That Do More Than “Basic”

Advanced facials and chemical peels are professional skin treatments designed to improve the look and feel of your skin beyond a simple cleanse-and-mask appointment. They are ideal when you want clearer texture, more consistent glow, refined pores, or a stronger renewal plan that supports long‑term skin health.

  • Advanced facials: customized treatments using professional products, targeted steps, and often upgraded options (like dermaplaning or SkinCeuticals protocols).
  • Chemical peels: controlled exfoliation using professional peel solutions to help refresh dull, congested, or uneven-looking skin.
  • Dermaplaning: a precision exfoliation technique that removes surface buildup and peach fuzz for a smoother finish.
  • Teen facial: age-appropriate support for young skin, breakouts, and early skincare habits.
  • Microchanneling: an advanced rejuvenation option (Pro-Cell) that supports a refreshed texture and tone over time.

If HydraFacial is your consistent “deep clean + hydration” foundation, advanced facials and peels are often where guests go when they want a stronger shift—like a seasonal reset, texture refinement, or a more targeted anti-aging plan.

2) How do these treatments work?

Customized Steps, Clear Outcomes

While the exact protocol depends on the service you choose, most of our advanced facial and peel visits follow a simple, client-friendly flow.

  • Consultation: we confirm goals, sensitivity, and timing
  • Prep: cleanse and assess current skin condition
  • Targeted exfoliation: peel, dermaplaning, or professional exfoliation steps
  • Focused treatment: customized masks, serums, or protocols (e.g., SkinCeuticals)
  • Advanced options: microchanneling or add-ons when appropriate
  • Finish + aftercare: protect the skin and set expectations for the next days

Chemical peels work by encouraging controlled exfoliation. Depending on the peel, you may experience minimal flaking or a more noticeable peel process. We will always set clear expectations before you book, especially if you have events coming up.

Dermaplaning provides a smooth, polished finish and can be especially helpful if makeup tends to cling to texture. It’s a popular choice for guests who want immediate “softness” without a big downtime commitment.

Pro-Cell Microchanneling is an advanced rejuvenation option that supports your skin’s natural renewal process. For many clients, it’s part of a series-based plan aimed at improving texture and overall tone over time.

3) Why get these treatments?

Because Skin Goals Need a Plan, Not Random Visits

Advanced facials and peels help when you want more than a quick refresh. They are practical, goal-driven services for guests who want visible improvement and a clearer path forward.

  • Brighter-looking skin: support glow and tone clarity
  • Smoother texture: refine roughness and surface buildup
  • Congestion support: help reduce the “clogged” feel
  • Anti-aging maintenance: support a more refreshed look over time
  • Better makeup application: especially after dermaplaning
  • Teen skin guidance: build healthy habits early with professional care

Many Danvers and North Shore guests book these services seasonally: an advanced facial when skin feels dull, a peel when texture needs a reset, dermaplaning before events, or microchanneling when they’re ready for a more premium rejuvenation plan.

Not sure what fits? A simple approach is: Hydration and clarity → start with HydraFacial. Texture and renewal → explore peels or dermaplaning. Advanced rejuvenation → consider Pro-Cell microchanneling.

How to Choose the Best Treatment Path

Facial, Peel, or Microchanneling?

If your skin feels dull, dehydrated, or reactive, a customized facial is usually the best first step. If tone and texture are your main concerns, a peel may deliver faster visible change. If you are focused on longer-term rejuvenation and refinement, microchanneling can be a stronger strategic option.

At Spalenza Spa, we prioritize matching intensity to your current skin state instead of over-treating. That approach helps clients in Danvers build results they can maintain, not just short-term improvement.
